# Executive Assessment Report

1. Cover information: client, assessment name, dates, consultant, confidentiality.
2. Executive summary: top outcomes, risks, and decisions needed.
3. Assessment purpose: why the work was performed.
4. Scope: teams, backlogs, repositories, releases, interviews, and exclusions.
5. Methodology: evidence review, interviews, maturity scoring, and human review.
6. Current-state summary: delivery operating model and constraints.
7. Strengths: capabilities to preserve.
8. Critical findings: risk-rated findings tied to evidence.
9. Customer and business impact: value, commitment, cost, risk, and trust implications.
10. Maturity scorecard: area scores and overall maturity.
11. Recommendations: prioritized actions with owners and definitions of done.
12. 30-, 60-, and 90-day roadmap: sequenced implementation plan.
13. Risks and assumptions: unresolved dependencies and assumptions.
14. Metrics: current and proposed decision metrics.
15. Follow-on options: advisory, facilitation, implementation support, fractional leadership.
16. Appendix and evidence references: evidence inventory and traceability matrix.
